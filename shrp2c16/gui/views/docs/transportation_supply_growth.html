This file contains four values that allow the user to change the transportation supply in the region in the future year.

Freeway –is the percent increase in road lane miles including freeways, arterials and other facilities. By default, the transportation supply is assumed to grow in line with population increase; therefore a value of 1 indicates growth in proportion with population growth. A value less than 1 indicates that there will be less freeway lane mile supply, per person, in the future. A value of 1 indicates faster freeway expansion than population growth.

Arterial - is a similar value to freeway except that it measures arterial lane mile growth. It is also proportional to population growth.

Bus – is the percent increase in transit revenue miles per capita for bus. It behaves in a similar way to the freeway and rail values in that a value of 1 indicates per capita revenue miles stays constant.

Rail - is the percent increase in transit revenue miles per capita for rail. This encompasses all rail modes, from light rail through to commuter rail.
